The Gap Is Structural (Income vs Home Prices)

When home values rise faster than wages, the constraint becomes structural: families can't save enough cash or clear debt-to-income ratios — especially first-time buyers and workforce households. Traditional DPA helps, but it often functions as episodic funding rather than a durable mechanism that scales with the market.

Policy Momentum Is Real, But Kentucky Needs "Supply + Access"

The budget signal is clear: Kentucky wants housing outcomes at scale. But Kentucky's next constraint will be delivery capacity — the ability to deploy quickly, track impact, and keep funds working year after year.
What Kentucky Is Emphasizing
